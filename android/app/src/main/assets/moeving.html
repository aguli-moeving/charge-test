Behind The Scenes — Our Corporate Meet Turned Into a Fun Meet with Colleagues (The Fun Part)

We are not a team because we work together, we are a team because we respect each other – Abigail Johnson

The corporate meetings come with an agenda to meet and discuss the fundamentals of the business model. When it is a remote team, it becomes crucial to introduce the team to the company’s vision and mission.

So, keeping the agenda in mind, the company planned a two-day retreat for the tech team at our Bangalore Office. We had informative sessions where we discussed logistics, charging infrastructure, and organized field visits to our charging stations that were followed by a round of questions from the team.

The retreat was hosted by Ashish, Technical Program Manager at MoEVing and we highly appreciated the efforts he put in making this a successful retreat.

Was that all we did? NO!

Let’s read together and relive the journey to Bangalore with us to know how we end up having the most memorable professional trip of the year!

When Virtual Team Met In Reality

As a remote team, we occasionally meet over video calls. But, the connection you require to build a professional and a sort of personal relationship with colleagues seemed missing.

The virtual setup does not allow you to spend more than an hour (it’s still more) on the video to talk about your day-to-day activities. Most often, you will discuss work, or sometimes about the current happenings in your surroundings, and that’s all. You feel short on words and conversations as what do you discuss next?

That’s one disadvantage of virtual working environments.

The hesitation, nervousness as we converse on video calls disappeared when we met for the first time in Bangalore. From there, our journey to understand and know one another begins, and it’s a never-ending story that continues after we are back to the virtual setup.

The Meeting Time!!!

Drum Roll Please!!! It’s the most awaited moment for all of us, and yes, we were overflowing with excitement.

I and Ashwini met at the airport and had an adventurous ride from the airport to the office that entails another story. But, in the 2 hours drive, we talked as if we had been friends for so long.

Visiting the office and meeting people seems unreal amid COVID, but we managed to meet, and, honestly, it was the best decision.

We met and introduced ourselves to the managers and other colleagues. The warm welcome made us feel comfortable in a space that doesn’t feel too professional to restrict you and hesitant to voice your opinions.

We continued our casual conversations at lunch at a very exotic Italian restaurant. If you are visiting Bangalore, I would recommend you to visit Chianti Koramangala. From dishes to drinks, everything was just scrumptious.

Later, we did have informative sessions with the team about the business after lunch at the office. It feels nice to see the freshness that each team member brought with their unique personality.

Field Trips to Charging Station

What’s fun without some field trips and excursions? During our time from office to charging station, we used to talk, discuss various topics, and exchange our thoughts. (It’s like reminiscing the school trips we used to have) Here, we saw different sides of each team member that remained hidden behind the screen.

We visited the ground-level operations and tried to understand the operations and where we can better utilize the technology. Also, the visit to the charging station helped us relate to our work more closely.

So, that was the end of our retreat, and we are back to our places? No! There’s more to it.

The Highlight of the Trip — Flight Rescheduled (What?)

As the day came to an end, we had to catch a flight later at night. We were all sitting at a tea shop having cheese sandwiches and lemon tea. No one wanted to leave.

Unaware, I said, I wish I could not go back today. Within a moment, Ashish got my flight rescheduled, and I kept wondering if it was really happening.

With my flight rescheduled, we got another flight rescheduled as well, so we could go back together.

But, we already checked out from the hotel, we have no place to stay! What do we do now?

Then, Ashish let all of us have fun and spend the night at his home as we were to catch an early morning flight. It was a night to remember where we were all sitting together, eating, dancing, and having fun, yet sometimes having serious conversations.

That’s how our trip experience was!

Dynamic, Lively, and Wholesome!

Let’s also ask our colleagues how they felt about the trip.

  • Jaya Jha, Chief Product Officer

The manager, the mentor to whom we can reach out for every problem that we face whether it is about the product or even your day-to-day tasks. She will listen to you and help you out with her incredible solutions.

“I was worried sick in my heart, although I didn’t show it, that how the team members would get along with each other having only interacted online before. But when I saw them together, they looked and behaved like just like any other office friends and colleagues who know each other very well. That was heartwarming and also a huge relief. It reasserted my belief that remote can work and still foster companionship and team spirit among people.”

  • Ashwini Guli, Senior Software Engineer

Ashwini is the most cheerful and loving girl around here. This girl can make you feel at home with her presence. Whatever it is, you can talk to her and feel good than ever!

“After working with the team for so long, it was a real delight to see people in person. The video calls we’d been doing really helped break the ice between the teammates.

Meeting people and getting to know them on a personal level is always fun, and meeting my colleagues was just that. We broke bread together, had parties and made memories. There was fun, laughter and general merriment seeing the team which is going to stay with me for a long time.

We got an opportunity to understand the ground-level operations. Understanding their work and seeing their efforts made me feel like we are a whole team of moEVing who believe in the vision of getting EV into the Indian Market. I left Bangalore feeling proud of the work we’ve done and inspired for the future.”

  • Ashish Kumar, Technical Program Manager

The host of the retreat and the problem solver of the team. All thanks to him, we enjoyed the trip to its fullest and we appreciate how patient you were throughout our trip even if we were late for meetings. (Sorry Ashish, we will make it up for you next time!)

We started off with a remote team which was spread across the country. We did a great job building our first version of app while understanding the business and use cases at the same time remotely. But for us to work closley with business we had to sync our thinking. Hence, the get-together.

In a short span of time when we were together we understood each other’s capability. It helped us to bond at a personal level. I am personally proud of myself to know my team at this level. This is certainly going to help us build our product at a warp speed. Look forward to more of these team-building activities.

  • Ramya T R, Senior Software Engineer

After six months of working virtually and only seeing my teammates on my laptop with fancy backgrounds, it was nice to finally meet them in person. Even if for a few hours, it was fun to work together in person, and without the cliche precursors of “Can you hear me?” and “Can you see my screen?”

We visited a client hub managed by MoEVing, some of their operations was handled manually and it was interesting to see all the opportunities where we can use technology to make the lives of our users better.

The visit to charging station that we launched was perhaps the most exciting because it was also related to the project we are working on currently.

The most important outcome for me was the bond that we shared those three days and the camaraderie that we carried back with us. I still feel the connection in our virtual meetings.

  • Anuroop Reddy, Product Designer

The one who talks less and listens more but when he begins to talk, it’s all interesting and good stuff. One should listen to his stories, they are damn funny. No doubt, you will have a great time talking to him.

I was like why not, atleast I will meet my colleagues in person. It was completely a nice experience to meet my internet colleagues, have official meetings, not to mention more unofficial meetups too. The field visits to the charging stations that aligns with tech operations, heads-up talks by marketing, charging infrastructure, etc., all of these gave me a good impression about MoEVing, the leaders who share the knowledge and experiences. I was in no doubt why will MoEVing be the next big thing.

  • Shaunak Sirodaria, Product Analyst

Analyst for a reason because he got the eyes of a critic. His curiosity to know everything makes him perfect for the job. He is full of fresh ideas and will try to find out a solution to any problem if you ask him.

Meeting and bonding with colleagues after working remotely for almost 2 years feels really satisfying.

All thanks to Ashish for organizing it and managing all the work and fun sessions extremely well. We, as the tech team were already well acquainted to each other through video calls but meeting the extended Bangalore based team was really warm and welcoming!

  • Venkatesh Seshadri, GM Software — QA

The Two-Day Professional Retreat to Bangalore (The Informative Part)

MoEVing started as a remote team, and since then, we haven’t had a chance to meet our colleagues or understand the operations by being physically present. So, it was decided to let the tech/product team visit the Bangalore office to understand ground-level operations and business models with clear insight.

If you haven’t read the first part, we shared our experience with you and how we have blended together as a team. You can read it right here.

The retreat was fun. But, the major part of the retreat included the informative sessions to help connect the tech team with the operations, management, and business model of the company.

Let’s discuss in brief how the trip was planned, executed, and what we learned about the operations.

Insightful Office Sessions

On the day of our arrival in Bangalore, we had some sessions set up in the office. The first session was with the business team to help us understand the basics of logistics and the electric vehicle industry.

Being an informative session, it cleared out many doubts we had related to the business model we are following and what to expect from the company in the future.

Next, we had an hour session with the management and maintenance team on the charging infra product. The interesting part of the session was the product we are creating aligned with this model.

The charging product is essential for the consumers, and the product team works with both the charging product and the consumers. So, it was necessary to align the strategies and the end goal to provide a satisfactory customer experience.

The Product Team will be the first point of contact to the consumers, so we should get the feel and touch of the business. Hence, the session was to help understand the product team how to connect the consumer and tech side of the business. We were briefed about the recent developments and the future plans for the product.

Compelling Field Trips

MoEVing Ground-Level Manual Operations

The next day was dedicated to the field trips. The field trips offer you a more comprehensive understanding of the manner the company operates and how each operation takes place.

First, we went to Fresh2Home where MoEVing runs its complete operations. The operations are done manually. Here, they get orders on their panel from where they will get an order and deliver it to the respective customers.

They run two types of deliveries, slots and express deliveries that include a mix of operation of milk and groceries deliveries. Furthermore, the process, as we said, is manual. They receive the order on the panel, they check whether the order is available or not, if available, they accept it and get an invoice along with the bar code. The barcode is attached to the order, the rider will scan it, and once it is delivered to the customer, they will mark it delivered. They will get a confirmation delivery message via SMS.

If you are thinking about how segregation and distribution of orders work, let us explain it to you.

Each shift has a supervisor who takes care of the segregation and deliverables. They will check what the items and shipments that need from the warehouse are and if it is matching with the order or not. For every order, there is a bar code that needs to be scanned before being taken out for delivery.

It was interesting to see how manual operations are planned out with discipline and order.

MoEVing Charging Hub

Later, we went to the first charging hub launched in Bengaluru to understand how it is managed and operated. There are 15 charging points available for the EVs to charge up.

The interesting part was that we are working on a similar product to include in our product. So, it gave us a very informative insight.

We have different chargers from different vendors. There were sockets provided for the electric vehicles to charge. Some chargers require an RFID card to start the charging, and the colors on the machine will determine the charger availability.

For example:

Green: Available

Blue: Maintenance

Green and Blinking: Charging

If the charger displays green color, that means it is available to plug your vehicle, and it will start charging. Once the electric vehicle has fully charged, it will automatically cut off the electric energy.

Now we want to take it one step forward. We want to create a product where it becomes convenient for riders to charge their vehicles.

Here, our field trip ended, and also our small professional retreat.

The Closing Session

Jaya Jha, our Chief Product Officer, hosted the closing session. She says she is an introvert and not good with small talk, she has successfully managed a remote team with absolute grace.

It was the most inspiring and motivational talk of the day. It will motivate you to work with dedication and determination to set an example that MoEVing provides the best quality product, the best culture, and the best opportunities for your career growth.

In her closing session, she pointed out three important determiners to create a healthy company environment.

  1. Culture

As we are in the early stages of the company, we have the liberty to build a culture and set an example for the future employees of how we engage and how we work.

How do we build a culture that’s beneficial for everyone?

Working responsibly and taking responsibility for the tasks assigned

Collaboration with the team while working independently

Be predictable about your availability

Communicate proactively across all forums

2. Growth

As a startup, we have so much to do that growth is a given. We will develop new products, build new features, bring more employees to help us grow. So, there is growth.

However, growth is not a gift, but it is incidental. For example, you know what you have learned, but you need to figure out what else you can learn to grow your career and simultaneously, the company. In the technical environment, Ashwini, a web developer becoming a mobile developer within a week is considered growth.

However, one should know when to step up and grab the opportunity.

3. Work

Each team member has dedicated roles and tasks assigned to maintain work continuity. We keep in mind that our work should not hamper or discredit the value of others’ work.

To maintain quality with speedy delivery, we need to figure out the value of the work we are delivering.

How do we do that? By Prioritizing the ideas and tasks.

Prioritization can happen in your daily tasks. You plan your day and prioritize tasks according to their value and priority. By doing so, you have a clear understanding of how the prioritization can benefit the target audience and the company. So, it is important to reflect on the decisions you make while prioritizing your work.

Good principles of working and culture help you manage and make the remote team work.

So, let’s build a team of talented and enthusiastic people to create our own learning culture and create a successful product for the users’ benefit.

Are you ready? We Definitely Are!!!
